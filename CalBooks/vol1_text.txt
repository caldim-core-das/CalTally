1. Introduction & Program Overview
1.1 Purpose of This Document
This volume is the Business Architecture layer of a nine-volume Enterprise Architecture Blueprint for a cloud accounting and business-management platform positioned as a functional peer to Zoho Books. It establishes the business capability model, stakeholder personas, value streams, and scoped module catalog that every downstream artifact - the BRD, SRS, FRS, Database Design Document, UI/UX Specification, API Design Document, Security Architecture, Testing & QA Specification, and Deployment & DevOps Guide - must trace back to.
Enterprise architecture programs at this scale fail most often not from a lack of diagrams but from an absence of a shared, unambiguous definition of "what the business does" before anyone designs "how the system does it." This document exists to remove that ambiguity. Every capability, persona, and value stream defined here becomes a first-class, numbered reference (e.g. BC-04.2, VS-03, PER-06) that later volumes cite directly, so a requirement in the SRS or a table in the DDD can always be traced back to a business reason for existing.
1.2 Relationship to the Full Blueprint
The full blueprint is being produced in phases, one architectural domain at a time, with review and sign-off between phases so each subsequent volume builds on agreed, stable foundations rather than assumptions. The nine volumes and their dependency order are:
The BRD, SRS, and FRS are intentionally not listed as separate volumes: in this blueprint their content is absorbed into Volumes 1-2 (business and functional scope) and Volume 9 (interface-level detail), which avoids the duplication that typically causes an SRS and an architecture document to drift out of sync in real enterprise programs. If a standalone SRS/FRS/BRD deliverable is still required for procurement or compliance reasons, Section 12 of this document shows exactly how to extract one from the numbered items here.
1.3 How to Read This Document
Section 4 (Business Capability Model) is the master index - every other section refers back to a capability ID such as BC-07.
Section 6 (Module & Feature Scope Catalog) is the working scope document - treat it as the object of change control going forward.
Section 7-8 (Value Streams & Process Flows) contain Mermaid diagram source blocks. They are intentionally kept as editable text rather than static images so they can be pasted directly into any Mermaid-compatible renderer, version-controlled, or regenerated as the design evolves.
Section 11 (Traceability Matrix) is what Vol. 2 onward will cite - do not renumber capability IDs after this document is approved without a corresponding renumber pass across all volumes.

2. Business Context & Product Vision
2.1 Vision Statement
Deliver a cloud-native, multi-tenant accounting and business-operations platform that lets small and mid-sized businesses run receivables, payables, banking, inventory, projects, and compliance from one system of record - matching the functional depth of Zoho Books while being architected, from day one, for API-first extensibility and horizontal scale.
2.2 Competitive Frame
The product is scoped against three incumbents whose feature sets define the addressable functional baseline: Zoho Books (primary reference - see Section 5 module catalog, derived from its published feature set as of July 2026), QuickBooks Online, and Xero. The competitive intent is functional parity on core accounting, receivables, payables, banking, and inventory within the first two release phases, with AI-assisted workflows (Section 5, BC-18) and open API/integration architecture (Volume 5) as differentiation vectors rather than table-stakes features.

13. Open Questions & Assumptions
13.1 Assumptions Made in This Draft
Target initial markets are US, India, and EU (drives BC-11/BC-12 scope) - confirm or adjust before Vol. 5.
Payroll (BC-10) is scoped as Release 3 given its heavy per-country statutory complexity - confirm this is acceptable given business timeline.
"Enterprise-class" is interpreted as multi-tenant SaaS with per-tenant data isolation, not single-tenant on-prem deployments. If on-prem/self-hosted is required, Vol. 4 and Vol. 7 scope changes substantially.
AI features (BC-18) are scoped as a differentiator, not MVP-blocking - confirm priority relative to core accounting parity.
13.2 Open Questions for Stakeholder Sign-off
Which countries must be supported for tax compliance at MVP vs. later releases?
Is a native mobile app required at MVP, or is a responsive web app sufficient for Release 1?
Should the accountant/bookkeeper multi-client experience (PER-03) be a Release 1 requirement, given it materially affects the tenant/org data model in Vol. 4?
What is the expected initial tenant scale (100s vs. 10,000s of organizations) - this directly sizes the infrastructure targets in Vol. 7?
Is white-labeling / reseller packaging in scope for any edition?
